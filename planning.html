Survival Classes

I will need to learn basic survival skills, outdoor first aid, etc before hiking the Appalachian Trail. Below are a list of classes I plan on taking before starting my thru hike.

  1. Wilderness Survival 101
  2. Certified Wildnerss First Aid
  3. Wild Edible and Medicinal Plants of New England
  4. Animal Tracks and Signs
  5. Wilderness Navigation (map and compass / GPS)

What I Will Eat / Drink

Below are a list of some of the common things I will pack when initially preparing and replenishing my food supply as well as a link to more ideas.

  1. Trail Mix / Nuts, seeds, nut-based bars
  2. Dried or freeze dried fruits and veggies
  3. Energy bars, chews, or gels
  4. Granola or granola bars
  5. Ready-made tuna salad pouches
  6. Whole-grain tortillas
  7. Poultry, salmon, or meat jerky
  8. Peanut butter
  9. ...More

I will be packing 3 liters of clean water in my Camelbak Hydration Pack for the week - when I run out of this, I will be using a LifeStraw water purification system to drink from renewable sources such as brooks and mountain streams.

Getting My Body Ready

A 2,190 mile hike will take a tole on my body. In order to prepare and get my body ready for such excersise, I will do the following:

  1. Hike 5-10 of the New Hampshire "48 4,000 footers" before I leave for my thru hike
  2. Take at least two 2-3 day backpacking trips
  3. Take at least one 7-10 day backpacking trip
  4. Excersise regularly, focusing on cardio and building leg muscle
